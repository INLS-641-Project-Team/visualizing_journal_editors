About This Project

The world of publishing is frustratingly opaque. While we can get an idea of who works for journals, we are often unaware of connections between editors and journals. Because of that, we may miss an understanding of the “shape” of the world of publishing. This brings to mind important issues about fairness and equity in publishing. If we can’t see how the world of publishing is connected, we can’t begin to grasp what inequity exists and what bias may exist. Further, we have to acknowledge the challenges faced by those unfamiliar with scholarly communication in understanding the nature of publishing. The thread connecting all of this can be found in a lack of clarity around what institutions and individuals are involved in publishing and to what degree they interact with each other. Our aim here is to provide the beginnings of an exploration of this issue in the hopes of teasing out which institutions and individuals are over-represented in publishing.
